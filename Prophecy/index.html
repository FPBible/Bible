How to Read Prophecy on This Site
All prophetic language on this site is read from the far side of fulfilment. “Coming,” “near,” “soon,” “day,” “hope,” and “until” are covenantal, not indicators of delayed physical events. Prophecy announces divine action within history — its fulfilment is recognised in Christ and the establishment of the New Creation.

This hub exists to anchor every fulfilled claim made across the Bible section. Whenever a passage alludes to prophecy, it must link back to one of these fulfilled frameworks. If a prophecy is implied and not linked, the page has failed its standard.

---

Core Fulfilled Prophecy Frameworks

  • Matthew 24 — The End of the Age
    Jesus’ covenant judgment discourse. Explains “coming on the clouds,” “this generation,” and temple destruction as first-century fulfilment, not modern speculation.
  • Luke 21 — Days of Vengeance
    Parallel to Matthew 24, emphasising Jerusalem, not the end of the planet. Frames judgment as covenantal reckoning.
  • Daniel — Kingdom, Son of Man, and Time
    Explains symbolic beasts, the Son of Man, and time-statements fulfilled in Christ’s enthronement.
  • Isaiah — New Heavens, New Earth, New Humanity
    Shows covenant-creation language, Zion imagery, and peace metaphors fulfilled in the New Covenant.
  • Ezekiel 37 — Resurrection as Restoration
    Valley of dry bones explained as covenant restoration, not biological resurrection prediction.
  • Psalms — Enthronement and Rule
    Messianic kingship, judgment language, and “rule from Zion” fulfilled in Christ’s reign.
---

New Testament Prophecy Re-Readings

  • 1 Thessalonians 4 — Presence, Not Escape
    Explains “caught up,” “meeting the Lord,” and resurrection language as royal arrival imagery.
  • 2 Peter 3 — Heavens and Earth
    Shows covenantal “creation” language, dissolving the myth of planetary destruction.
  • Revelation — Symbol, Judgment, Victory
    Apocalyptic imagery decoded through Old Testament symbolism and first-century fulfilment.
